Tenerife Carnival is one of the most important cultural events on the island and is widely considered one of the best carnivals in the world. Every year, thousands of visitors travel to Santa Cruz to enjoy parades, comparsas, murgas and spectacular shows filled with music, creativity and fantasy. For weeks, the island comes alive with rhythm, costumes and an energy that is hard to match. However, beyond the vibrant atmosphere, Tenerife Carnival can also be experienced from a different perspective, combining the festive spirit with moments of relaxation by the sea.

Tenerife Carnival: history and tradition

Tenerife Carnival dates back to the 18th century, when pre-Lenten celebrations included masked balls and lively street festivities. Over time, these traditions evolved into the large-scale celebration we know today.

The Queen’s Gala, murgas and comparsas competitions, the Coso parade and the symbolic Burial of the Sardine are among the most iconic events. While Santa Cruz hosts the main celebrations, the carnival spirit extends across the island, including the south.

Tenerife Carnival is not just a celebration; it represents cultural identity and reflects the island’s open and creative character. This is why it attracts both national and international visitors seeking a truly unique experience.

Enjoy the carnival and unwind afterwards

Although Tenerife Carnival is synonymous with celebration and vibrant energy, not every traveller wishes to stay in the heart of the festivities. Many prefer to combine the excitement of the carnival with a peaceful setting where they can unwind after a full day of events.

This is where the south of the island, and especially La Caleta, becomes the perfect alternative. Located in Costa Adeje, this area offers a far more relaxed atmosphere, with seaside promenades, quality restaurants and a slower pace that contrasts with the intensity of the capital during carnival season.

From La Caleta, it is easy to reach the main Tenerife Carnival events and then return to a serene space to rest.
