A course with history and Atlantic views

Inaugurated in 1998 and designed by renowned Spanish architect José Gancedo, Golf Costa Adeje was built with deep respect for the land’s original topography, once covered by terraced banana plantations. That agricultural heritage is still visible today in the course’s layout, where stone walls and natural slopes add character and authenticity to every hole.

The result is a course celebrated for both its scenic beauty and its technical challenge: 18 holes, par 72, combining wide fairways with strategically placed greens. Throughout the game, players are accompanied by breathtaking ocean views, with La Gomera on the horizon and postcard-perfect sunsets to end the day.

Two courses for every level

Golf Costa Adeje offers two courses designed to suit all levels of play.

Championship Course (18 holes, par 72)

A technical and visual challenge that demands precision, strategy, and a keen understanding of the wind. It’s common to see professional players training or enjoying a round in this exceptional setting.

Los Lagos (9 holes, par 33)

A more relaxed course, ideal for beginners or those seeking a shorter game without compromising on quality. Several natural lakes enhance the landscape, creating a serene and refreshing environment.

Both courses share the same philosophy: to deliver a complete golf experience in a setting of incomparable natural beauty.

Host to world-class tournaments

Since its opening, Golf Costa Adeje has hosted prestigious international tournaments such as the Spanish Open and events from the European Tour. These competitions have cemented its reputation worldwide and positioned Costa Adeje as a top destination for high-level sports tourism.

Its world-class facilities, meticulous maintenance and Tenerife’s privileged climate — with an average annual temperature of 23 °C — make it possible to play golf 365 days a year. For this reason, both professionals and amateurs from around the globe choose this area to train, compete or simply relax in a luxurious environment.
